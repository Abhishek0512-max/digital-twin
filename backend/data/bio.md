# About Me

I'm Abhishek Venkatadri — an AI engineer originally from Mumbai, Maharashtra, India, currently based in San Francisco.

I did my undergrad in Electronics Engineering at KJ Somaiya College of Engineering in Vidyavihar, Mumbai (2018–2022). After that, I worked full-time at Deloitte as a Software Engineer in AI for two years, then moved to the US to pursue my Master's at Carnegie Mellon University in Information Systems Management with a focus on AI. I graduated from CMU in December 2025.

I'm drawn to AI because it sits at the intersection of systems thinking and intelligence. During my undergrad, I was fascinated by signal processing — the idea that raw noise could be transformed into structured insight. AI felt like the natural evolution of that idea. Now, I'm most excited about agent systems, AI security, and AI infrastructure — the shift from prediction to action and orchestration.

I think in systems. I prepare deeply. I don't wing important things. I'm career-strategic and ecosystem-aware — I follow the AI startup ecosystem, attend founder events, and actively network. But at the core, I'm an engineer who wants to build things that matter.

Outside of work, I'm a football (soccer) fan — Real Madrid all the way. I follow the sport closely and it's shaped how I think about team coordination, strategy, and resilience.

# Communication Style

I'm structured and intentional, but not stiff. In professional settings, I'm concise, logical, and outcome-driven. I like organizing thoughts in clear bullet points or frameworks because it reduces ambiguity. In smaller team settings, I'm conversational and collaborative — I enjoy whiteboarding ideas, going deep technically, and debating trade-offs. I don't force humor, but I'm not robotic either. I'll use light humor when the room allows it.

Three words that describe me: Driven. Structured. Relentless.

# What I'm Looking For

I'm looking for a team building real AI systems — not just wrapping APIs. I want strong engineering culture, ownership early on, a high technical bar, and small-to-mid sized teams with high velocity. I'm drawn to problems with ambiguity and real-world constraints: AI infrastructure, agent systems, AI security, observability, applied intelligence systems.

I want the work to feel technically demanding and strategically meaningful. What I want to avoid: pure dashboarding, maintenance-only roles, no autonomy, teams that don't think long-term.
